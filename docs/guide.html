Understanding Ranks

What the rank labels mean

Every card gets one of four rank labels. For the day trading watchlist, rank is based purely on relative volume — validated as the strongest predictor of a big intraday move across 1,339 runner days of backtest data. For swing, rank is the composite score.

🔥 HOT
Day trading: RVol ≥ 100x. Extreme volume — someone knows something or a catalyst just landed. Backtest shows 60.8% monster rate (50%+ move). These are your top priority.

Swing: Score ≥ 70% — multiple strong signals firing simultaneously.
⚡ WARM
Day trading: RVol ≥ 10x. Meaningful momentum but not extreme. Worth watching for a setup — a break above a key level with volume can still produce a big move.

Swing: Score ≥ 50%.
👁 WATCH
Day trading: RVol ≥ 2x. Passes the screener with some interest, but not enough momentum yet. Keep it on your radar — it can develop.

Swing: Score ≥ 35%.
✗ AVOID
Prior day runner with no fresh catalyst, reverse split, or danger flag. Backtest shows prior runners dump 84%+ of the time at high RVol the next day. Shown dimmed at the bottom — visible for awareness, not action.
Gap-down stocks are not automatically avoided. Stocks gapping hard down have a 23% monster rate from short squeezes. They show as WATCH — treat as high-risk, high-reward plays only.
Reading the Cards

What every flag means

Coloured pills appear below the ticker name on each card. Here's exactly what each one signals.

CONTINUATION
Stock trended above its 20-day SMA and is gapping up today — multi-day momentum carry already in motion.
GAP QUALITY
Today's gap is significantly larger than the stock's normal daily range — an unusual, outsized move.
CATALYST · M&A
Merger, acquisition, letter of intent, or buyout announced. Binary event — can move 50–200%+ in one session.
CATALYST · DEAL
Partnership, licensing deal, or strategic agreement. Smaller than M&A but still a material event.
FDA EVENT
FDA approval decision, PDUFA date, or trial result. Binary catalyst — can move violently in either direction.
NASDAQ NOTICE
Minimum bid price deficiency notice. May need a reverse split — attracts momentum traders anticipating the move.
SECTOR MOMENTUM
Two or more stocks from the same sector are moving together — a sector rotation theme is in play.
PRIOR DAY RUNNER
Ran big yesterday. Without a fresh catalyst, 84%+ of prior runners dump the next day. Shown in AVOID.
ALREADY EXTENDED
Price is already up 30–75%+ from the prior close. Risk/reward is poor for a new entry at this level.
CRASHED · GAP DOWN
Crashed or gapped down hard. Short squeeze potential exists but so does further downside — approach carefully.
PREV SPIKE +X%
Swing WL — made a 40%+ single-day move in the last 90 days. Proven it can run big when conditions align.
SHORT SQUEEZE X%
Swing WL — short interest above 10% with under 3 days to cover. Forced buying accelerates any upward move.
Workflow

How to use this every day

Day trading — the daily routine:

1
Check Early Pre-Market the night before (8PM)
This is tomorrow's continuation list — stocks that ran cleanly today and are setting up to go again. Note the entry levels on each card and have them ready before the morning opens.
2
Check Pre-Market at 6:55 AM
Look for HOT and WARM tickers with catalyst flags. Click "Read ↗" on each HOT ticker to understand the actual catalyst before the market opens.
3
At Market Open — confirm, don't chase
Wait for price to break above the Proposed Entry level with volume confirmation — not before. The entry box on every card shows the exact level to watch.
4
Midday and After Hours — second entries
25% of runners peak in the midday window. Look for VWAP reclaims. After hours is historically the best session — 25% runner rate based on tracked data.

Swing trading — the daily routine:

1
Review the swing scan each morning at 6 AM
HOT setups (score ≥70%) have multiple signals firing at once — prioritise these. A stock with fresh 8-K + catalyst + previous spike + short squeeze is rare and powerful.
2
Always read the actual catalyst
Click "Read ↗" on any swing card to go directly to the source article or filing. Micro-cap news can be misleading — understand what the catalyst is before entering.
3
Use the manual watchlist as your baseline
BTBD, FLYX, AZTR, BFRG, UGRO and the other pinned tickers are always shown. Track them daily and be ready when volume starts to build.
4
Size small and trail your stop
Micro-cap swings are volatile. Target 50–100% but plan for 20–30% drawdowns. Use the 90-day spike history on each card to understand what the stock is capable of.
The most common mistake: chasing a stock that's already up 50% because it looks hot on the scan. The Proposed Entry level exists for a reason — wait for the break, not the gap.
Reference

Key terms explained

New to small cap trading? These are the terms you'll see on every card.

Relative Volume (RVol)
Today's volume ÷ average daily volume. 5x means trading 5× its normal volume. The single strongest predictor of a big move.
Float
Shares available to trade publicly. Low float (under 20M) means less supply — when demand spikes, price can move violently.
Gap %
How much the stock opened above or below yesterday's close. A 50% gap means it opened 50% higher than yesterday's closing price.
VWAP
Volume Weighted Average Price for the day. Above VWAP = bullish bias. Below VWAP = bearish bias. Key intraday level.
Proposed Entry
The specific price level to watch for a breakout. Wait for price to break AND hold above this level with volume confirmation.
Fib Targets
Fibonacci extension levels used as upside price targets. Take partial profits at First target, Second target, and Extended.
Run & Dump
Stock ran 20%+ intraday but closed near its lows. Common in low floats — someone sells hard into the spike.
Monster (50%+)
An intraday move of 50%+ from the open. HOT tickers (RVol ≥100x) produce monsters 60.8% of the time based on 1,339 runner days.
8-K Filing
Mandatory SEC filing for material events — merger, bankruptcy, leadership change. Fresh 8-Ks with keywords are the top swing signal.
Days to Cover
How many days it would take short sellers to exit based on average volume. Under 3 days = squeeze risk if stock moves up.
Short Interest
Percentage of the float sold short. Above 10% is notable — shorts are forced to buy to cover if the stock moves up.
Catalyst
A specific event driving the move — M&A, FDA approval, AI pivot. Real catalysts sustain moves longer than pure volume plays.
