Two watchlists, two timeframes

The system runs two separate scanners pulling from Finviz Elite's real-time data. They share the same universe — low float, small cap stocks under $20 — but they're built for different strategies.

📈 Day Trading Watchlist

Intraday momentum plays. Stocks moving right now with high relative volume and catalysts. Runs 5 times daily. Hold time: minutes to hours.

📊 Swing Trading Watchlist

Multi-day momentum plays targeting 50-100% moves. Scored on 8-K filings, previous spike history, short squeeze setups, and catalyst quality. Hold time: days to weeks.

Not financial advice. These watchlists identify setups for manual review. Always confirm on a live chart, check Level 2, and size appropriately before entering any trade.

Understanding Ranks

What the rank labels mean

Every card gets one of four rank labels. For the day trading WL, rank is based purely on relative volume — the single strongest predictor of a big intraday move validated across 1,339 runner days of backtest data. For the swing WL, rank is based on the composite score.

🔥 HOT

Day trading: RVol ≥ 100x. These stocks are moving on extreme volume — someone knows something, or a catalyst just hit. Backtest shows 60.8% monster rate (50%+ move) at this threshold. Swing: Score ≥ 70% — multiple strong signals firing simultaneously.

⚡ WARM

Day trading: RVol ≥ 10x. Meaningful momentum but not extreme. Worth watching for a setup — if it breaks a key level with volume it can still run big. Swing: Score ≥ 50%.

👁 WATCH

Day trading: RVol ≥ 2x. Passes the screener, some volume interest, but not enough momentum yet. Keep it on the radar — it could develop. Swing: Score ≥ 35%.

✗ AVOID

Prior day runner with no fresh catalyst, reverse split, or crashed gap-down. Backtest shows prior runners dump 84%+ of the time at high RVol the next day. Shown dimmed at the bottom for awareness only.

Gap-down stocks are NOT avoided. Stocks gapping down hard (-20%+) have a 23% monster rate due to short squeezes. They show as WATCH so you can see them — but treat them as high-risk, high-reward plays only.
Reading the Cards

What every flag means

Each card shows flags — coloured pills below the ticker name. Here's what each one means:

CONTINUATION
Stock trended above its 20-day SMA and is gapping up today — multi-day momentum carry.
GAP QUALITY
Today's gap is significantly larger than the stock's normal daily range — unusual move.
CATALYST · M&A
Merger, acquisition, letter of intent, or buyout announced. Binary event — can move 50-200%+.
CATALYST · DEAL
Partnership, licensing deal, or strategic agreement announced.
FDA EVENT
FDA approval decision, PDUFA date, or trial result. Binary catalyst — can move violently either direction.
NASDAQ NOTICE
Minimum bid price deficiency notice from NASDAQ. Stock may need to do a reverse split — attracts momentum traders.
SECTOR MOMENTUM
Two or more stocks from the same sector are moving together — sector rotation theme in play.
PRIOR DAY RUNNER
This stock ran big yesterday. Without a fresh catalyst, 84%+ of prior runners dump the next day. Shown in AVOID.
ALREADY EXTENDED
Price is already up 30-75%+ from the prior close. Risk/reward is poor for a new entry — the move may be largely done.
CRASHED · GAP DOWN
Stock crashed or gapped down hard. High risk — short squeeze potential exists but so does further downside.
PREV SPIKE +X%
(Swing WL) This stock made a 40%+ single-day move in the last 90 days — proven momentum mover.
SHORT SQUEEZE X%
(Swing WL) Short interest above 10% with less than 3 days to cover — forced buying if it moves up.
Workflow

How to use this every day

Day trading workflow — the morning routine:

1
Check Early Pre-Market (8PM the night before)
This is tomorrow's continuation list — stocks that ran cleanly today and are setting up to go again. Note the entry levels (today's high) and have them ready for the morning.
2
Check Pre-Market at 6:55 AM
Look for HOT and WARM tickers with catalysts. The 8AM window produces 9% of all runner highs — your pre-market scan catches setups before that spike. Read the news headline for each HOT ticker.
3
At Market Open (9:30 AM) — confirm, don't chase
Use the Market Open scan to see what's still moving. Wait for a break above the proposed entry level with volume confirmation — not before. The entry box on each card shows the specific level to watch.
4
Midday and After Hours — second entries
25% of runners peak in the midday window (12:30-3:30PM). Look for stocks that pulled back to VWAP and are reclaiming it. After hours (3:30PM+) is historically the best session — power hour setups and HOD breaks.

Swing trading workflow — weekly routine:

1
Review the swing scan each morning at 6 AM
HOT setups (score ≥70%) have multiple signals firing — prioritise these. Check the score grid on the card to see which signals triggered. A stock with fresh 8-K + catalyst + previous spike + short squeeze is rare and powerful.
2
Click "Read ↗" to verify the catalyst
The news pill links directly to the source article. Always read the actual filing or announcement before entering a swing position. Micro-cap news can be misleading — understand what the catalyst actually is.
3
Use the manual watchlist as a baseline
BTBD, FLYX, AZTR, BFRG, UGRO and the other manual tickers are always shown. These are known momentum movers in this price range — track them daily and be ready when volume picks up.
4
Size small, trail your stop
Micro-cap swings are volatile. Target 50-100% but plan for 20-30% drawdowns. Use the 90-day spike history to understand the stock's range. If it spiked 80% before, a 50% target is realistic. If it only spiked 40%, temper expectations.
The most common mistake: chasing a stock that's already up 50% because it looks hot on the scan. The entry box and proposed entry level exist for a reason — wait for the break, not the gap.
Reference

Key terms explained

Relative Volume (RVol)
Today's volume divided by the average daily volume. 5x means trading 5x its normal volume. The single strongest predictor of a big move.
Float
The number of shares available for public trading. Low float (under 20M) means less supply — when demand spikes, price can move violently.
Gap %
How much the stock opened above or below yesterday's close. A 50% gap means it opened 50% higher than yesterday's closing price.
VWAP
Volume Weighted Average Price — the average price weighted by volume for the day. Key intraday level. Above VWAP = bullish bias, below = bearish.
Proposed Entry
The specific price level to watch for a breakout. Usually the prior day's high or a key resistance level. Wait for price to break AND hold above this level with volume.
Fib Targets
Fibonacci extension levels used as upside price targets. First target, second target, and extended target — take partial profits at each level.
Run & Dump
Stock ran 20%+ intraday but closed near its lows. Common in low floats — someone sells into the spike. The session high was achievable but you had to exit quickly.
Monster (50%+)
An intraday move of 50% or more from the session open. HOT tickers (RVol ≥100x) produce monsters 60.8% of the time based on 1,339 runner days of data.
8-K Filing
A mandatory SEC filing when a company has a material event — merger, bankruptcy, executive change, etc. Fresh 8-Ks with merger/AI/crypto keywords are the highest-scoring swing signal.
Days to Cover
How many days it would take short sellers to buy back their shares based on average volume. Under 3 days = squeeze risk if the stock moves up.
Short Interest
The percentage of the float that is sold short. Above 10% is notable — if the stock moves up, shorts are forced to buy to cover, accelerating the move.
Catalyst
A specific event or announcement that drives the move — M&A deal, FDA approval, AI pivot, earnings beat. Stocks with real catalysts have higher sustained moves than pure volume plays.
